# CSC-114 Assessment Rubrics
## Artificial Intelligence I - Grading Standards

**Course**: CSC-114 AI I
**Instructor**: Drew Norris
**Term**: Spring 2026

---

## Philosophy: Process Over Product (30% Process Grading)

All assignments are graded on:
- **70% Technical Implementation**: Does the ML work correctly?
- **30% Process Quality**: Did you manage the work professionally?

---

## Weekly Assignment Rubric (25 points typical)

### Technical Implementation (17-18 points)
- **Excellent (90-100%)**: All requirements met, code runs without errors, results well-analyzed
- **Good (80-89%)**: Most requirements met, minor errors, adequate analysis
- **Satisfactory (70-79%)**: Basic requirements met, some errors, minimal analysis
- **Needs Improvement (<70%)**: Missing requirements, significant errors, incomplete

### Process/PM (7-8 points)
- **Excellent (90-100%)**: All PM artifacts complete, professional quality, evidence of learning
- **Good (80-89%)**: Most PM artifacts present, adequate quality
- **Satisfactory (70-79%)**: Basic PM artifacts, minimal effort
- **Needs Improvement (<70%)**: PM artifacts missing or incomplete

---

## Sprint Assignment Rubric (50 points typical)

### Technical Deliverable (25 points)
**Code Quality (10 pts)**
- Runs without errors (3 pts)
- Well-commented and readable (3 pts)
- Follows best practices (2 pts)
- Efficient implementation (2 pts)

**Functionality (10 pts)**
- All required features implemented (5 pts)
- Features work correctly (3 pts)
- Edge cases handled (2 pts)

**Analysis & Documentation (5 pts)**
- Results clearly interpreted (2 pts)
- README updated (2 pts)
- Visualizations effective (1 pt)

### PM Execution (20 points)
**Sprint Planning (5 pts)**
- Sprint goal clear and achievable (1 pt)
- Capacity based on velocity (1 pt)
- Stories properly formatted and estimated (2 pts)
- Acceptance criteria defined (1 pt)

**Daily Execution (8 pts)**
- Daily standups consistent (8+ of 10 days) (4 pts)
- Sprint board managed (stories move through workflow) (2 pts)
- Burndown chart maintained (2 pts)

**Sprint Artifacts (5 pts)**
- All GitHub Issues created (2 pts)
- Sprint planning document complete (2 pts)
- Professional presentation (1 pt)

**Velocity & Learning (2 pts)**
- Velocity tracked accurately (1 pt)
- Evidence of learning from previous sprints (1 pt)

### Improvement Bonus (5 points)
- Sprint completed on time (2 pts)
- All stories done (no carryover) (2 pts)
- Clear improvement from previous sprint (1 pt)

---

## Retrospective Rubric (25 points)

### Honest Reflection (10 points)
**What Went Well (3 pts)**
- Specific examples (not generic) (1 pt)
- Multiple perspectives covered (1 pt)
- Both technical and process wins (1 pt)

**What Didn't Go Well (4 pts)**
- Identifies real problems (2 pts)
- Specific, not vague (1 pt)
- Balanced (not all negative or all positive) (1 pt)

**Root Cause Analysis (3 pts)**
- Digs deeper than surface symptoms (2 pts)
- Uses "5 Whys" or similar (1 pt)

### Actionable Improvements (8 points)
**Action Items (5 pts)**
- Specific and measurable (2 pts)
- Assigned to owner with due date (1 pt)
- Realistic and achievable (1 pt)
- Address root causes (1 pt)

**Start/Stop/Continue (3 pts)**
- At least 2 items in each category (2 pts)
- Specific process changes (1 pt)

### Estimation Learning (4 points)
- Analyzes estimation accuracy (2 pts)
- Updates reference estimates (1 pt)
- Shows learning trajectory (1 pt)

### Professionalism (3 points)
- Well-organized and readable (1 pt)
- Growth mindset evident (1 pt)
- Celebrates wins while acknowledging challenges (1 pt)

## Grading Policies

### Late Work
- 10% deduction per day late (up to 3 days)
- After 3 days: 50% maximum grade
- Sprint deliverables cannot be more than 1 week late (defeats sprint purpose)

### Revision Policy
- Assignments < 70%: One revision opportunity within 1 week
- Must meet with instructor to discuss improvements
- Maximum grade after revision: 85%

### Process Grading Philosophy
**Process points are NOT**:
- Busywork
- Optional extras
- Less important than technical

**Process points ARE**:
- Evidence of professional work habits
- Practice for capstone (CTS-289)
- Skills employers value
- Foundation for managing complex projects

### Velocity Expectations
**Sprint 1**: No expectations, establishing baseline
**Sprint 2**: Within 20% of Sprint 1 velocity
**Sprint 3+**: Velocity should stabilize (Â±10% variation is normal)

### Standups Expectations
- Minimum 8 of 10 days per sprint
- Brief (3 questions format)
- Honest (saying "struggled" is better than saying nothing)

## Academic Integrity

**Collaboration Encouraged**:
- Discussing concepts
- Helping debug (not writing code for others)
- Sharing resources
- Peer review

**Not Allowed**:
- Copying code
- Sharing solution notebooks
- Using complete solutions from internet
- AI-generating entire assignments

**PM Artifacts**:
- Must be YOUR sprint, YOUR velocity, YOUR retrospective
- Generic/template responses obvious and unacceptable
