üî¨ NLP Techniques You'll Implement

  • Tokenization: Breaking text into words, sentences, or subwords
  • Text Cleaning: Removing noise (HTML, punctuation, special characters)
  • Normalization: Lowercasing, stemming, lemmatization
  • Bag of Words (BoW): Count-based text representation
  • TF-IDF: Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency weighting
  • Word Embeddings (Concepts): Understanding Word2Vec approach (implementation optional)
  • Sentiment Analysis: Positive/negative/neutral classification
  • Text Classification: Multi-class document categorization
  • Handling Imbalanced Text Data: Techniques for uneven class distributions

üí° Drew's Sprint 5 Optimization Guide

  • Use your velocity data: Don't guess capacity. Look at Sprint 2-4 average and commit to that.
  • Estimation should be dialed in: If you're still wildly off on story points, revisit your reference estimates.
  • Streamline your rituals: Sprint planning should take 30 min max. Standups: 2 minutes. Retro: 45 min.
  • NLP text preprocessing is 80% of the work: Get tokenization and cleaning right, and the model is easy.
  • "This movie was sick!": Context matters. NLP is harder than it looks because language is ambiguous.
  • TF-IDF beats Bag of Words for most tasks: It downweights common words like "the" and "a".
  • Start with simple sentiment analysis: Positive/negative classification is easier than multi-class.
  • Optimize your process, not just your code: Faster commits, better documentation, cleaner PRs.
