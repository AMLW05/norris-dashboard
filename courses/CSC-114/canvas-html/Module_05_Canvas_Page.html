üí° Drew's Neural Network Implementation Guide

  • Start simple: 1-2 hidden layers, 32-64 neurons each. Complexity later.
  • Expect slow training: Neural networks take time. Start with small epochs (10-20) to test, then scale up.
  • Your first model will perform poorly: That's step 1. Optimization is where the art happens.
  • Watch for overfitting: Training accuracy high, validation accuracy low? You're overfitting. Add dropout or L2.
  • Learning rate matters: Too high = unstable. Too low = slow. Start with 0.001 and adjust.
  • Visualize training curves: Plot loss and accuracy. They tell you everything about training dynamics.
  • Keras documentation is your friend: Don't memorize APIs. Learn to read docs efficiently.
  • PM should be automatic by now: If you're spending time figuring out sprint mechanics, you're missing the point.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Sprint 3 Pitfalls

  • Treating PM as checkbox exercise: I can tell if you're just going through motions. Authentic execution only.
  • Not tracking training time: Neural networks train SLOWLY. Budget your time accordingly.
  • Ignoring overfitting: High training accuracy doesn't mean good model. Check validation metrics!
  • Random hyperparameters: Don't just guess. Make deliberate changes and track results.
  • Skipping retrospective depth: "Sprint went fine" isn't a retro. What SPECIFICALLY can you improve?
  • Over-engineering architecture: 10 hidden layers won't make your model better. Start simple.
  • Not using early stopping: Let the model tell you when it's done training. Don't hardcode epochs.
