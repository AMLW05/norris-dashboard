# Weekly Planning Template
## CSC-113: Planning Your Module Work

**Use this template at the start of each 2-week module**

---

## Why Plan Your Week?

**Without a plan**:
- "I have 2 weeks" turns into "OH NO IT'S DUE TOMORROW"
- You don't know what to work on first
- You forget small tasks until it's too late
- You feel overwhelmed and don't start

**With a plan**:
- You spread work across available time
- You know exactly what to do each day
- You catch problems early (can ask for help!)
- You feel in control and make progress

---

## The 2-Week Module Cycle

CSC-113 modules run for **2 weeks** (assignments due Friday of Week 2).

**Week 1**: Learn + Start
**Week 2**: Build + Finish

### Week 1 Pattern
- **Monday/Tuesday**: Read materials, watch videos
- **Wednesday/Thursday**: Start assignment, create issue/branch
- **Friday**: Work session 1 (get 30-40% done)

### Week 2 Pattern
- **Monday**: Work session 2 (get to 70% done)
- **Tuesday/Wednesday**: Work session 3 (finish 100%)
- **Thursday**: Review, test, polish
- **Friday**: Submit by 11:59 PM

## Time Blocking Tips

### Find Your Best Working Time
**Morning person?** Schedule hard tasks before noon.
**Night owl?** Save focus work for evening.
**Lunch breaker?** Use 30-minute lunch sessions for small tasks.

### Block Specific Times
‚ùå **Vague**: "I'll work on this sometime Tuesday"
‚úÖ **Specific**: "Tuesday 2:00-3:30 PM, CSC-113 work session"

### Protect Your Time Blocks
- Set phone timer/alarm
- Close Discord/social media
- Tell roommates "I'm working for 1 hour"
- Reward yourself after (snack, walk, game)

## Handling Unexpected Events

### "I got sick and missed 3 days"
**Solution**:
1. Look at your plan - what's critical?
2. Cut optional tasks (extra polish, nice-to-haves)
3. Ask for extension if needed (do this EARLY, not day-of)
4. Adjust next week's plan to prevent cramming

---

### "The assignment took way longer than expected"
**Solution**:
1. Check your task breakdown - did you miss something?
2. Ask for help - maybe you're doing it wrong way
3. Use weekend buffer time
4. Update your estimates for next module

---

### "I totally forgot about the assignment"
**Solution**:
1. DON'T PANIC
2. Create plan for remaining time
3. Focus on core requirements (skip extras)
4. Submit what you can
5. Learn from this - set calendar reminders for next module

## Common Planning Mistakes

### ‚ùå Mistake 1: "I have 2 weeks, I'll start next week"
**Result**: Panic on Day 13
**Fix**: Start Week 1 Monday. Even 20 minutes counts.

---

### ‚ùå Mistake 2: Planning every detail too far ahead
**Result**: Plan changes, you feel behind
**Fix**: Plan Week 1 in detail, Week 2 in general. Adjust Week 2 based on Week 1 progress.

---

### ‚ùå Mistake 3: Not scheduling WHEN you'll work
**Result**: "I'll do it later" ‚Üí never happens
**Fix**: Block specific times. Treat them like appointments.

---

### ‚ùå Mistake 4: No buffer time
**Result**: One small delay ruins entire plan
**Fix**: Always add weekend buffer or Thursday "catch-up" time.

---

### ‚ùå Mistake 5: Ignoring your actual schedule
**Result**: Plan says 3 hours Tuesday, but you have work/practice/family stuff
**Fix**: Look at your REAL calendar first. Plan around commitments.

## Drew's Planning Philosophy

> **"A plan is not a prison. It's a tool."**

**Your plan will change**. That's normal and OK.

**Signs of a GOOD plan**:
- ‚úÖ You know what to do each day
- ‚úÖ You catch problems early (can adjust)
- ‚úÖ You feel in control, not overwhelmed
- ‚úÖ Work is spread out, not crammed

**Signs of a BAD plan**:
- ‚ùå So detailed it's rigid (no flexibility)
- ‚ùå Based on perfect conditions (no buffer)
- ‚ùå You avoid looking at it (feels like nagging)
- ‚ùå It doesn't match your real life

**Adjust your plan as you go. That's what professionals do.**
