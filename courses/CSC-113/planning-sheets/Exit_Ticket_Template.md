## Why Exit Tickets Matter

**For You:**
- Reinforces learning through reflection
- Tracks your progress over time
- Identifies patterns (what works, what doesn't)
- Builds metacognition (thinking about thinking)

**For Drew:**
- Shows your thought process
- Helps identify common struggles
- Guides what to emphasize in class
- Demonstrates growth beyond just code

**For Your Grade:**
- Part of process grading (not just product)
- Shows engagement and effort
- Counts toward participation
- Demonstrates continuous improvement

## Drew's Advice

### Common Mistakes:
‚ùå Rushing through it ("Nothing blocked me, everything's fine")
‚ùå Being vague ("I learned stuff")
‚ùå Skipping questions you can't answer
‚ùå Doing it all at once at semester end

### Success Strategies:
‚úÖ Be honest (blockers are learning opportunities!)
‚úÖ Be specific ("I learned the difference between features and labels in ML")
‚úÖ Use it to actually reflect, not just check a box
‚úÖ Complete it immediately after finishing module
‚úÖ Review past exit tickets to see your growth

### The Real Goal:
Exit tickets aren't busywork. They're **process documentation**.

In the real world:
- Standups ask "What shipped? What's blocking? What's next?"
- Retrospectives ask "What did we learn? What should we improve?"
- Documentation requires explaining your thinking

You're practicing professional reflection habits.
