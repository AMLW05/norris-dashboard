üí° Andrew's Tips for Success

  • Risk registers are living documents: Don't just update them once and forget. Check them weekly. New risks emerge constantly.
  • High-probability + high-impact = must act now: Use the probability/impact matrix to prioritize. You can't mitigate every risk.
  • Mitigation strategies need owners and dates: "We'll monitor this" isn't a plan. Who's monitoring? When will they report?
  • DoD is negotiable, but must be explicit: Teams define their own DoD. Just make sure everyone agrees before the sprint starts.
  • Acceptance testing is done by users, not developers: If possible, have stakeholders test. If not, role-play as a user.
  • Quality metrics without action are vanity metrics: Test coverage of 80% means nothing if those tests don't catch bugs. Focus on meaningful measures.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls in Module 6

  • Treating risks as problems: Risks are potential problems. They haven't happened yet. That's why you can prevent them.
  • Writing vague risks: "The project might fail" isn't a risk. "API vendor could deprecate endpoints we depend on" is a risk.
  • Assuming DoD is "code works": DoD includes tests written, docs updated, code reviewed, deployed to staging, etc.
  • Ignoring low-probability, high-impact risks: A 5% chance of complete data loss still needs a mitigation plan.

üì£ Andrew's Note

By Week 11, your project should feel real‚Äîwhich means risk feels real too. Maybe your stakeholder stopped responding. Maybe you realize your original design won't scale. Maybe you're running out of time. All of these are risks you should have identified earlier, but if you didn't, now's the time to address them.

Quality is not inspection‚Äîit's prevention. Testing at the end of a sprint is too late. Build quality into your workflow. Write tests as you code. Review your own work before submitting it. Make quality a habit, not a phase.

If you get to Week 12 and nothing in your risk register has changed, you're not updating it. Projects are dynamic. Your risk awareness should be too.
