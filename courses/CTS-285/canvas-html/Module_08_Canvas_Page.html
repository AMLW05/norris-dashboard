üí° Andrew's Tips for Success

  • Start with the problem, not the solution: Why does this project matter? Hook your audience with the "why" before explaining the "how."
  • Rehearse your demo 10 times: Things will go wrong during live demos. Practice until you can recover gracefully from any error.
  • Tell a story, not a timeline: "We spent 16 weeks building a system" is boring. "We discovered users needed X, so we pivoted our design" is interesting.
  • Show, don't tell: Instead of "Our system has a dashboard," show the dashboard and explain what insights it provides.
  • Own your mistakes: If asked "Why didn't you do X?", don't get defensive. Explain your reasoning and what you'd do differently.
  • Have a backup demo: Record a demo video. If your live demo crashes, switch to the video seamlessly.
