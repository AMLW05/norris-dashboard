üî¨ Hands-On Activity: SDLC Simulation Lab (120 minutes)

What you'll experience:

You'll participate in a live simulation where teams build a simple product (like paper airplanes or LEGO structures) using three different approaches: Waterfall (all planning up front), Iterative (with checkpoints), and Agile (with rapid sprints and feedback).

What You'll Practice:

  • Comparing delivery speed and quality across SDLC models
  • Understanding how customer feedback impacts development
  • Experiencing the frustrations of late-stage changes in Waterfall
  • Seeing the value of iterative delivery and adaptation

Reflection Questions:

  • Which approach delivered the best product? Why?
  • When did you receive customer feedback in each model?
  • How did your team adapt to changing requirements?

üí° Andrew's Tips for Success

  • Don't overthink project selection: Choose something you're genuinely interested in. You'll be working on this for 16 weeks, so pick a problem you want to solve.
  • SDLC models aren't "right" or "wrong": Each has appropriate use cases. Focus on understanding trade-offs, not memorizing definitions.
  • The Agile Manifesto is only 68 words: Read it multiple times. It's the foundation of everything we'll do with Scrum and user stories.
  • Simulations feel silly, but they work: Lean into the paper airplane exercise. The lessons are real, even if the product isn't.
  • Talk to real stakeholders: If you're proposing a project for an actual organization, interview someone who would use your system. Real insights beat guessing.
